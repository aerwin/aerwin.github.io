Earlier this month, I was in Basel, Switzerland to present at [Cloud Foundry Summit Europe](https://www.cloudfoundry.org/event/europe-2017/). Normally, I'd write about the conference (and I still hope to do that in the near future), but this post is going to diverge from my normal technology-focused topics. Instead, I'm going to describe my chance encounter with one of the locals that turned into one of the most fascinating conversations I've ever had.

I had some time to kill the day before the conference, and decided to venture out and get some lunch. I headed out from my hotel (Swissotel -- TODO: get official name and link) into a nice, cool autumn day (nice to actually have fall temps, leaves turning, etc.). I noticed a restaurant (the Movenpick [TODO: need the special character for the "o"] Brasserie Baselstab Restaurant) that appeared to have a healthy number of customers and some open outdoor seating. So, I grabbed a seat and ordered some lunch. Just before my food arrived, an older woman approached my table and said something to me in German and chuckled. I gave a bit of a quizzocal look and said "Sorry, I don't understand." She then said in English, "I made a joke. I asked if I could sit down and told you not to worry because I don't eat human flesh." Of course, I said sure, but admittedly was a little worried what I was getting into.

She proceeded to order some coffee and french fries, and we started talking.

My food arrived and she said "eat, eat"

her stuff arrived a little later

said something about japanese concentration camp... and I was confused... she went on to explain her father was Dutch and ran a factory of some kind in Indonesia. When World War II broke out in the Pacfic, the Japanese rounded up folks from the allied nations and put them in concentration camps. She spent the first 5 years of her life there.

she also said something about the next several years after that were war

she mentioned a time when she was sick and vomitted the very small amount of food she was given... her mom made her eat the vomit because she didn't want her daughter to starve to death

mentioned sexual stuff with Japanese soldiers who would go after the young girls


brought up the Bridge of the River Kwai -- and it was nothing like how bad it really was in the concentration camps


I tried a few times to ask how she got through that with a positive outlook on things

she believed in god which seemed amazing to me
